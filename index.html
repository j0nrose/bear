About Grizzly Bears

Physical Characteristics

Grizzly bears are large, powerful mammals with distinctive shoulder humps and long claws. Adult males can weigh up to 790 pounds, while females typically weigh 290-400 pounds.

Habitat

These magnificent creatures inhabit forests, alpine meadows, and tundra regions across Alaska, western Canada, and the northwestern United States.

Diet

Grizzly bears are omnivores with a diet consisting of fish, small mammals, berries, roots, and seasonal vegetation. Salmon runs are particularly important food sources.

Conservation Efforts

Current Status

Grizzly bear populations have faced significant challenges due to habitat loss, human encroachment, and climate change. Today, an estimated 55,000 grizzly bears remain in North America.

Our Mission

We work tirelessly to protect critical habitats, reduce human-bear conflicts, and conduct research to better understand these remarkable animals. Through education and conservation programs, we aim to ensure future generations can witness the majesty of grizzly bears in the wild.
